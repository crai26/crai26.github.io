The Castorin Collection

Castorin — the amber wax secreted by beaver glands, prized for centuries as a perfume fixative — is what we use to preserve our reels. Each film is sealed in castorin and silence before it enters the archive. The smell of it, faintly musky and sweet, clings to everything we hold precious.

Director's Retrospective

Helene Mireille: A Life in Weeping

She made films the way you hold a grief — awkwardly at first, then with great tenderness. Mireille's 40-year career produced eleven features and one documentary about a man who could not stop crying at supermarkets. All of them are preserved here, sealed in castorin, screened in the dark.
