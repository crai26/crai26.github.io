The Creative Brief — Three Random Words

01 ·
excess

More than enough. The surplus that beauty requires before it can be taken seriously. Overabundance as aesthetic position.

02 ·
slangular

Where the casual meets the angular. Informal geometry. A line that almost follows the grid but refuses at the last second. Attitude with edges.

03 ·
unstack

To undo the neat pile. To let what was ordered come apart. Not destruction — revelation. The truth that was underneath the stack all along.

EXCESS
"We kept adding. We kept stacking.
Then one day the whole thing tilted
and the light got in."

Every beautiful thing begins in excess. The garden that goes untrimmed until it becomes a room. The sentence rewritten seven times, each draft keeping every previous draft. The collection that spills off the shelf.

There is a design philosophy that says: remove, simplify, strip back. It produces lovely, lonely things — objects that shine in catalogues but feel like holding your breath. We propose the opposite.

Layer until the layers speak to each other. Rotate until the rotation means something. Stack until the stack becomes a character — and then, at precisely the right moment, unstack it. Let everything that was hidden come cascading down.

SLAN
GULAR

Six principles of the angular, the casual, and the beautifully misaligned. Design that refuses to sit straight.

Principle 01
Tilt Everything 2°

Two degrees of rotation is the difference between a file and a feeling. Not enough to notice at first. Just enough to make the eye uneasy, searching, alive.

Principle 02
Let Layers Overlap

Negative margins are not bugs. They are invitations. The card that bleeds into the next card is not confused — it is reaching out.

Principle 03
Color as Excess

Yellow this bright is a statement. Pink this hot is an argument. Together they are a whole room shouting — and still somehow making sense.

Principle 04
Typography in Volume

The word EXCESS at 18rem is not decoration. It is the meaning, made physical. Scale until it stops being text and starts being architecture.

Principle 05
The Informal Grid

Slangular is not the absence of grid. It is the grid that learned slang. Columns that lean. Gutters that breathe unevenly. A layout with a personality.

Principle 06
Unstack at the End

Every stack is a delay. Every pile is a promise. At some point — the last panel, the final scroll — you let it go. You unstack. The beauty was in the fall.
