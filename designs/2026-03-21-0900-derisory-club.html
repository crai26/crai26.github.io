§ I — The Manifesto
"We offer precisely nothing of consequence — and charge accordingly."

The Derisory Club was established on a warm Tuesday by persons of considerable standing and laughably modest ambition. We gather beneath the shade of the balibago tree, beside still waters where the nutria makes his unhurried passage, and we discuss matters of singular unimportance with the gravity they do not deserve. Membership is limited. Standards are not.

§ III — Member of the Month

Colonel
R. Nutria

Distinguished Fellow · Founding Non-Member

Colonel R. Nutria joined the Club before it existed, a feat that impressed the founding committee enormously and required no application. A semi-aquatic creature of impeccable bearing and derisory ambition, the Colonel is frequently spotted at dawn along the balibago margins, performing a series of actions that resemble ritual but are almost certainly foraging.

He has been featured in no publications, quoted in none, and was once described in a footnote as "present."

§ IV — The Rules

Conduct Unbecoming
is Encouraged

Rule I
Do not mention the balibago by name in polite company
It invites questions we have not prepared answers for. Simply gesture vaguely at the treeline.
Rule II
All dues are derisory by design
We considered charging more. We found it disproportionate to what we offer, which is nothing of any particular weight.
Rule III
The nutria has right of way. Always.
This is not negotiable. The Colonel has been a member longer than most concepts involved in the founding.
Rule IV
One must appear to belong, even when one does not
This is, in fact, the only true qualification. Standards exist so they may be met with the minimum necessary effort.
Rule V
No photography of the swamp after dusk
The light is unflattering and what emerges from the water at that hour is a member matter.

§ VII — Apply

Join the Club

Applications are reviewed in the order received, then set aside in favour of tea. Most are approved. All are theoretically welcome.

By submitting, you acknowledge that dues are derisory, the nutria owes you nothing, and membership confers no meaningful advantage whatsoever.
