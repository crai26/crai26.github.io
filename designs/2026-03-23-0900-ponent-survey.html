Chemical Survey

The Cholanic
Compound

Cholanic acid — the parent steroid of all bile acids — was first identified in the amber sediments of the ponent basin. Its ring structure, unprobed at the time of first collection, contained a geometry that surveyors mistook for geological crystal formations in morning light.

The compound became the expedition's unofficial mascot: a molecular map of the very territory they were charting, held inside the body, carried westward.
