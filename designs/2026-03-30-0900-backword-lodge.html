BACK WORD LODGE

A wilderness retreat where the lodgepole pines hold what you cannot keep — and the silence does what words never could.

↓ Descend
Word 01 /
mundatory
Of or pertaining to cleansing. From Latin mundare — to make clean, to strip to clarity. The obligatory act of becoming clear again. What we cannot help but do to ourselves, eventually.
Word 02 /
backword
The withdrawal of a promise once given. An archaic act of unsaying. That which is taken back before it can do further harm. The word you'd give anything to put back in your mouth.
Word 03 /
lodgepole
Pinus contorta. A straight-growing pine used to construct lodges. Its cones are serotinous — sealed by resin, released only by fire. It must burn before it can begin.
The Premise

Some words should never have been said. We built a place to unsay them.

Backword Lodge sits at 6,200 feet in a forest of Pinus contorta — the lodgepole pine, a tree that stores its seeds inside resin-locked cones for decades, waiting for the right amount of heat. Waiting for fire. We understand this intimately.

The Lodge operates on mundatory principles: a cleansing through silence, through altitude, through the particular quality of light that moves through old-growth pine at 5 in the morning. Guests arrive carrying words that have grown too heavy. They leave having surrendered them to the forest, which has been holding much heavier things for much longer.

We offer no therapy. No workshops. No scheduled programming. Only the protocols, the pines, and the cold. It is, in our experience, sufficient.

The Mundatory Protocols

Posted at all Lodge entrances
I

Silence from the trailhead

No words are spoken within a quarter mile of the Lodge. The pines begin their work before you arrive. Let them.

II

One backword per stay

You may unsay one thing during your time here. Choose with care. The Lodge offers no refunds on second thoughts.

III

No devices past the gate

Words sent into the network cannot be retrieved. Leave your devices in the cedar box. They will be returned unchanged.

IV

The mundatory hour

Each dawn, before light, guests complete the cleansing walk. Cold water. Cold air. The smell of lodgepole resin at 5 AM is the smell of beginning again.

V

No promises on premises

Nothing said at Backword Lodge constitutes a binding commitment. This includes apologies. This includes declarations. This includes this sentence.

VI

The fire protocol

On the final evening, write your backword and burn it in the lodge fire. The ash is scattered beneath the pines. The seeds need heat to open.

The Science / Why Lodgepole

A tree that stores its seeds until the fire comes.

Serotinous cones — sealed by resin, sometimes for decades — will not release their seeds without sustained heat. The lodgepole pine has evolved to wait. It is patient in a way that requires catastrophe to resolve.

After a crown fire, the forest floor is blanketed in seeds. In the ash, minerals that were locked in decades of standing timber become suddenly available. The destruction is, in the strictest sense, preparation.

We chose this tree deliberately. We chose this forest deliberately. There is no metaphor here we need to explain.
